Enterprise Data

Big Data is a Big Deal

Scalable data management is not a new problem, but it has grown over the years and Enterprise-ready Data Management requires even more as things evolved.

A History

The Hadoop platform, some foundational components dating back to the late 1990s, was created to address this scalable data management problem in a cost effective way on any commodity hardware.
Arguably it hit critical mass when a small company called Yahoo! ran a production 700-node Hadoop Cluster in 2007.
Within a few short years Cloudera and Hortonworks were founded, Amazon started offering their Elastic MapReduce service, and Yahoo!'s small cluster hit over 42,000 nodes.

World-Renown

Growing Pains

Hadoop has helped solve some issues related to cost, distribution, and scale and is used around the world, though as the deployment of enterprise data was adopted additional challenges were presented.

Data Integration

B2B, B2C, B2B2C2U

As data drove more and more value and helped move IT from a cost-center to a revenue generating part of the business, integrations became mandatory.
Not simply between different teams or systems in your organization, but interchanges between businesses helped pave the way for the hyper-aware and hyper-active capabilties that now give us the ability to know within moments of when an Amazon package has been delivered.

Data Science

More than just Hot Dog/Not Hot Dog

With the additional integrated repositories of data and a scalable platform, analysts and scientists could now create and employ models that were previously technologically impossible.
This science can range from predictive measures on equipment, to social-economic patterns on a global scale, the tracking of public epidemics, exploration into the depths of the Genome, and personalization functions that improve engagement.

Data Security

Don't get Equifuxed

Data has accelerated business and the missions of many agencies, however as the value of commodities like data increase, so does the appeal to malicious parties.
Throughout the years, and even more so in the recent few, we've seen time and time again about an organization or agency that has been breached, and more often than not it's a case of bad Data Governance and Security instead of a dynamic exploit.
Now, imagine the number of systems breached and the amount of data that has been exfiltrated or tampered with that hasn't been detected or reported.
Organizations now more than ever have the requirement and often legal responsibility to keep their data campaigns secure.
