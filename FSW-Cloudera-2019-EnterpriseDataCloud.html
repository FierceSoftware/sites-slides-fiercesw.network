Enterprise Data

Big Data is a Big Deal

Scalable data management is not a new problem, but it has grown over the years and Enterprise-ready Data Management requires even more as technology evolved.

A History

The Hadoop platform, some foundational components dating back to the late 1990s, was created to address this scalable data management problem in a cost effective way on any commodity hardware.
Arguably it hit critical mass when a small company called Yahoo! ran a production 700-node Hadoop Cluster in 2007.
Within a few short years Cloudera and Hortonworks were founded, Amazon started offering their Elastic MapReduce service, and Yahoo!'s small cluster hit over 42,000 nodes.

World-Renown

Growing Pains

This popularly growing data platform had helped solve some issues related to cost, distribution, and scale and is used around the world, though as the deployment of enterprise data was adopted additional challenges were presented.

Often these different silos were disconnected, housed different kind of data, and rarely was a like format.
So what many had were snapshots of different points of data, disperate bits of insight, without a way to get the value of corellating that data.

Data Integration

B2B, B2C, B2B2C2U

As data drove more and more value and helped move IT from a cost-center to a revenue generating part of the business, integrations became mandatory.
Not simply between different teams or systems in your organization, but interchanges between businesses helped pave the way for the hyper-aware and hyper-active capabilties that now give us the ability to know within moments of when an Amazon package has been delivered.

Data Science

More than just Hot Dog/Not Hot Dog

With the additional integrated repositories of data and a scalable platform, analysts and scientists could now create and employ models that were previously technologically impossible.
This science can range from predictive measures on equipment, to social-economic patterns on a global scale, the tracking of public epidemics, exploration into the depths of the Genome, and personalization functions that improve engagement.

Cloudera DataFlow

One of the integral parts in Cloudera's Enterprise Data Cloud is Cloudera DataFlow.
CDF is intended to power your needs around marshalling data from the edge, how it flows through networks and systems, processing capabilities while the data is in motion, and analytics presented for every bit of streamed data for what, who, and where. All of this wrapped around a security governance policy.
With CDF you can quickly migrate your data or even just parts of it, to new geos, storage types, and so on. Enrich your data on fly, pulling in and aggrigating between multiple sources of data to deliver more rich analytics and more holistic vantage points. In case your SIEM (Security Information and Event Management) system is eating your budget, you can use CDF to cancel out the noise and stream only what's important, be that logs, actions, sensor feedback, or any other sort of data.
With CDF you can take data in from various sources, be that a data lake you have in a public cloud, your on-premise data warehouse, or even web-based streamed data such as social media streams. From there you can quickly organize, sort, and transform the streamed data while in flight, and set parameters and policies on where that data goes, such as another tool like Elastic or your SIEM.
In case you have a high volume of data to manage and stream, CDF can respond to millions of events a second with data of almost any size imaginable. Governance policies and life cycles are managed easily from the same secure and end-to-end encrypted drag-and-drop UI that you build your flows in, and if there isn't a stream processor already available in the expansive catalog it's rather easy to create one.
You have more than the Web UI as an interface, flows and their components are stored as files and can be edited manually or produced as part of an automation pipeline. With the version control offered by the registry in CDF, you can apply the same DevOps and CI/CD workflows that your other development teams benefit from.

Edge Management

CDF can offer more than just streaming of data, it can offer a way to govern the movement and weight of your data through your enterprise. This allows you to not only stream, transform, aggrigate, and enrich streaming data but also ensures that it only arrives at its intended destination.
Now, CDF is what helps Cloudera tackle the Edge to AI. With Minifi, you can stream data from small IoT devices at and beyond the edge. This can fit on megar IoT and Single Board Computing devices such as the original Raspberry Pi, to more hardened and weather proof real-time OS driven embedded sensors.

Stream Processing

Now as you're streaming this data in and out of your data lakes and in-between your edge devices, you'll run into some common problems. Some streaming solutions require you to move your data somewhere, store it in a specific way, in order to be used which wastes a lot of costly resources and most importantly - time. Often times this is time-series based data so the information is more crucial at the time of inception, which means your insights and analytics programs are operating after the fact instead of being able to respond immediately. And if you've got all that tacked then I'm sure you know and see every piece of data moving through your enterprise, right?
The solutions offered in CDF actually do tackle those challenges. With Kafka you have a robust and scalable Publisher/Subscriber queue that can respond to millions of streams. Apache Storm allows you to act and process upon those streams in real time while the data is still in flight without needing to offload and aggrigate onto a compute cluster. With the Streams Messaging Manager and the Streaming Analytics Manager you gain the ability to not only see the streams going through your system and their associated properties but you can analyze and visualize thoses streams as well. The Schema Manager extends powerful access and version control to the key components that make up your flow and provides a collaborative CI/CD workflow to accellerate your data campaigns.
These and other technologies come together to offer you the CDF platform, one of the key components in the EDC that harnesses all of your data, from Edge 2 AI.
Outside of the technology stack, a few things that set apart CDF is the assurance of Cloudera's 100% dedication to Open Source. As I mentioned before, there are a bevy of built-in stream processors, the number of which has almost doubled in the last 2 years due to the drive of the community and how easy it is to create new data processors that can work across multiple streaming analytics engines, with governance and management policies baked in.

Cloudera Platform Roadmap

Now for something a lot of our customers have been asking out, we'll be taking a quick look at how things are shaping up with the merger of these technology platforms.
Something being introduced with the new Cloudera EDC is a simple set of tools to manage your entire EDC. You'll get a single pane of glass to act upon your data lakes, polcies, and workloads.
Welcome to the next generation data platform from Cloudera. A single management plane to orchestrate, operate, and secure your whole platform, no matter if it's in the cloud, multiple clouds, on-premise, and even right down to the edge devices. Cloudera's SDX is a part of this platform that unifies data management for all your data and presents it without a stateful context that defines the security, governance, cataloging, and lifecycle polcies around the various data endpoints. This presents you with a data catalog that allows you to apply multi-disciplinary analytic models no matter where your data is. In the middle of this stack sandwich, we have the core functions that comprise the Enterprise Data cloud, be that streaming, manipulating, storing, accessing and analyzing, and creating new ML models, all on an open-source platform.
The merger of the two platforms from Cloudera and Hortonworks brought together some amazing technologies, but also presented some overlap.
A few of the noteworthy and most asked about changes - moving forward Cloudera will support the very user-friendly Cloudera Manager, Ranger will keep security checks in line, CloudBreak has been selected for automated and multi-cluster managed cloud deployments, and Hive on Tez is being promoted for it's rich integration with the YARN architecture.
Looking at the charts you can see that we're greatly benefiting from the merger of these platform since most changes are due to their merging of similar technologies into a simpler stack, or integrating complementary technologies. You'll be happy to know that you can run your Clouder Data Science Workbench Jobs right next to your Zeppelin Notebooks.
Now, does this mean that if you were operating the previous Cloudera stack, do you need to rip and replace your Cloudera Director with CloudBreak managed clusters? The answer is no, Cloudera is dedicated to supporting these softwares for a few years to come as their customers carve out their upgrade strategies.
So there's a slight change in form-factor and overarching architecture with the new Cloudera EDC.
As you saw from the screenshot earlier, the new EDC will feature a unified management control plane that's offered as a hosted service by Cloudera. If you are operating in a secure enclave, don't worry - there are options for disconnected environment as well.
It's through this new management console that you'll not only access your data, but also where you'd instanciate your Data Warehouses, Jobs and ML models, Data Engineering services and more.
SDX is the glue that brings your various data lakes together, and of course this can be run across any supported public cloud provider.
If you're not using a Public Cloud providers and are still working on premises or want to include these resources in your EDC, Cloudera offers a wide array of options.
As we've seen the industry adopt containers and orchestration platforms like Kubernetes, Cloudera has also developed a scalable architecture on Kubernetes so that your services and workloads can be scaled up and down, out and in, all on demand.
Now, you can run this on your own Kubernetes platform, or take the "batteries included" approach where Cloudera provides you with exactly what you need, with the Kubernetes sort of baked in, to quickly get up and running.
The use of Kubernetes as the underlying platform allows Cloudera's EDC to scale and run on any cloud or environment without the need to manage different hypervisors, images, and so on.
As Cloudera and Hortonwork's product catalogs and development road maps have converged, we now have the ability to run a unified data platform across our various environments and use a single management console to operate all of our EDC, governance, policies, and workloads enacted on any and all of your data, with the peace of mind knowing that you're operating a 100% open-source data platform that keeps you free from vendor lock-in or relying on cloud-specific APIs and services.
So what should you look for and keep in mind when sizing your own EDC?
Being able to interact with your data workflows as you would in traditional DevOps practices is key, but your platform components and workloads also need the ability to atomically scale and upgrade.
Try to steer clear of products and platforms that rely on one or specific closed environments or ecosystems. This keeps you from vendor lock-in and also allows you to deploy your data and workloads where cost and resources are most opportune.
Jumping in and out of control panels, different technologies that don't interoperate fluidly, and a lack of automation is what we're already faced with - a unified solution that can intelligently catalog, govern, and secure your EDC is paramount in being able to get up to speed and stay at that pace.
